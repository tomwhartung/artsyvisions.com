The images above are spiritual portraits, or Grojas.[1] They depict the personalities of crew members on the bridge of the Starship Enterprise on the Original Star Trek series.

Lieutenant Uhura is the starship's communications officer, Lieutenant Sulu is the ship's helmsman, and Ensign Chekov is the navigator. The final image is of their boss, Captain Kirk.

A Diverse Crew?

The crew is interesting because it is racially diverse during a time when American TV was for the most part all-white. Uhura is African, Sulu is Asian, and Chekov is Russian.

Their personalities are not very diverse, however. The images of Sulu's and Chekov's personalities show they are very similar.

Uhuru's image shows she is more emotional than Sulu or Chekov, but otherwise similar in temperament. It makes sense for her to be more emotional and the others more logical, because her position calls for her to work more with people, while Sulu and Chekov work more with the ship.

Star Trek TOS on DVD

These portraits are based on the DVD box set containing all 79 episodes of Star Trek: The Original Series. Each episode has been digitally remastered, and the results are beautiful.

The remastered disks include new special effects sequences, reworked with modern technology. The disks enable you to switch between the original and updated sequences while watching an episode. This unique feature is great fun to play with, but I did not fully appreciate it until after experiencing the commentaries.

As my good friends these days know, commentaries are one of my favorite features of watching any series or movie on DVD! I am a lifelong fan of art history, and I love watching these and hearing the behind-the-scenes stories of any show!

The special features include interviews with the people who worked on the special effects discussing the challenges they encountered, their priorities, and the compromises they had to make. The commentaries also include interviews with writers for the series, such as David Gerrold, who wrote The Trouble With Tribbles, and D. C. Fontana, Gene Roddenberry's secretary-turned-story editor and writer.[3]

Seeing the special features always make me want to rewatch the episodes in light of the new information. As I write this, I am rewatching the series for the third time, and can say I have definitely gotten my money's worth in buying this box!
