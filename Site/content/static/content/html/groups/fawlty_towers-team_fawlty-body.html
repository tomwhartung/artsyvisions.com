The images above are spiritual portraits or Grojas.[1] They depict the personalities of characters on the show Fawlty Towers.

Basil Fawlty and his wife Sybil run a fictional hotel named Fawlty Towers in southern England. Polly serves as the hotel's chambermaid, receptionist and waitress, and Manuel is the bellboy and waiter.

These spiritual portraits show that the team at Fawlty Towers is diverse and interdependent. Each team member contributes their own personal set of unique skills, making the team capable, robust, and resilient.

Basil's most distinctive quality on this team is his objectivity — represented by the Green in his spiritual portrait. The Yellow represents his openness, and this openness takes precedence. As the owner he is also ostensibly the boss, but in reality he is frequently distracted by schemes,[2] making him largely unsuited for the role.

Sybil's most distinctive quality on this team is her decisiveness — represented by the Red and Green in her spiritual portrait. As Basil's wife, her role is ostensibly to support him, but in reality her decisiveness makes her the boss.

Polly's most distinctive quality on this team is her idealism — represented by the Blue in her spiritual portrait. Her ability to think outside of the box enables her to be much more than a maid, receptionist, and waitress. Ultimately her creativity plays a critical role in assisting Basil execute and cover up his schemes.

Manuel's most distinctive quality on this team is his warmth — represented by the Yellow in his spiritual portrait. He is very likeable, relatable, devoted to Basil, and eager to help out any way he can.

The Yellow in Basil's portrait shows he can get bored easily, and the Green shows he's can be a schemer. The Red and Green in Sybil's image shows she's the decisive one in — and the real boss of — Team Fawlty.

The Blue in Polly's image shows she can be good at coming up with fresh ideas to help keep Basil from getting in trouble. The Red in her and Manuel's images show they're both amiable, and they show this by helping Basil when they can.

Full of Blue and Yellow, Basil’s portrait reveals his openness and a strong desire for stimulation.

His need to break free causes him to fall prey to Sybil's domineering manner, which in turn makes him emotional to the point of being manic.

Whether it’s trying to appeal to a higher class of customer, save a little money on construction costs, or place a bet on a horse after hearing about a sure thing, Basil’s carelessness, haplessness, and bad luck never fail to get the best of him. Despite Sybil’s best efforts – or quite possibly because of them – he time and again fails to learn a lesson, and as a result, hilarity ensues.

Full of Red and Green, Sybil’s portrait stands in stark contrast to Basil’s. It reveals her confidence, decisiveness, and a need to control others, but she is willing to actually lift a finger and help out – albeit begrudgingly – during emergencies.

Sybil’s mantra is “I know, I know,” and she is too busy fussing with her hair and nails to care for any of Basil’s schemes. If only Basil would just obey her every demand, and not stray into his hare-brained schemes, the Fawlty Towers universe would be perfect — and no doubt the show would be incredibly boring.

The contrast between Basil and Sybil’s personalities is just one of the aspects of the show that puts it in the #1 position on the BFI TV 100 – the list of the top 100 TV shows compiled by the British Film Institute in 2000.[3] The supporting characters – Manuel the linguistically challenged busboy, bellboy and outlet for Basil’s wrath, and Polly the maid, hostess, art student and primary voice of reason – each lend a hand in trying to keep the chaos Basil’s scheming causes from Sybil’s watchful, critical eye.

With Manuel and Polly helping them, Basil and Sybil form a unique team: Team Fawlty. Together the four of them are capable of handling almost any situation, including health inspectors, drunk contractors, Germans, possible marital infidelity, and even the occasional dead guest.

Working together, Team Fawlty is capable of just about anything – anything, that is, except making a simple Waldorf Salad.
