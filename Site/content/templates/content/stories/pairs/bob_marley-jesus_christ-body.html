The images above are spiritual portraits, or Grojas.[1] They depict the personalities of Bob Marley, a central figure in Reggae music, and Jesus Christ, the central figure in the Christian religion.

xNFx: Blue and Red

Both images contain a lot of Blue and Red, because both Bob and Jesus tend to be idealistic and compassionate.

In terms of the four-letter types made popular by the MBTI®, both are xNFx — indicating both of them exhibit Carl Jung's iNtuition and Feeling traits.

Blue — iNtuition

The Blue in these images represents the trait Jung and the MBTI call iNtuition. A person who is high in iNtuition tends to see things and events more in terms of ideas than facts.

Although Bob and Jesus were different in many ways, the idea of God plays a major role in both of their lives. For example, rather than singing about God, Bob uses the term Jah in songs such Forever Loving Jah[2] and Rastaman Vibration[2].

As far as I'm concerned, anyone who asserts there is a significant difference between God and Jah is just being disagreeable — and unnecessarily defensive and divisive.

This idea that a higher power exists is one reason they are more idealistic than realistic. Now, this does not mean that everyone who is iNtuitive believes in Jah or God or Allah, only that more pragmatic people tend to doubt the existance of something they cannot see, hear, touch, smell, or taste.

Red — Feeling

The Red in these images represents the trait Jung and the MBTI call Feeling. A person who is high in Feeling tends to be more emotional than logical, and tends to prefer working with other people to working with inanimate things.

Both Bob and Jesus were compassionate and cared a lot about other people. While Jesus demonstrated his compassion by healing the sick, Bob makes his opinion clear in songs like Jammin',[2] in which he claims life is worth much more than gold.

Although Bob lacked Jesus' ability to perform miracles, he sought to comfort people with songs such as Three Little Birds,[2] in which he sings that every little thing gonna be alright and Johnny Was,[2] in which he seeks to comfort a woman whose son had been shot down in the street and died, just because of the system.

The emotions that Red represents in these images are not always positive, but in this case it represents the love that Bob and Jesus have and express for other people.
