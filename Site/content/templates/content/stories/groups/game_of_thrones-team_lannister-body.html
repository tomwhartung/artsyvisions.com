The images above are spiritual portraits, or Grojas.[1] They depict the personalities of Tywin Lannister and his oldest children, Cersei and Jamie, who are twins.

Tywin, Cersei, and Jamie Lannister are primary protagonists — and the nemeses of House Stark — on the show Game of Thrones.

A Rational, Realistic Family

The Green that dominates all three of these images represents the Lannisters' preference for being rational and decisive. They do not hesitate to make demands of others, and they expect to be obeyed.

The Yellow in these spiritual portraits represents the Lannisters' realism — their preference for seeing the world in terms of facts rather than ideas. This is both natural and understandable because one very important fact is the Lannisters have a lot of money.

An Ambitious Family

Tywin: the hard ass. Not at all open, actually a control freak, and always right.

Cersei: more human than Tywin. Passionate about her children, and little else.

Jamie: more open and hence more human than his father and sister, and his father berates him for it.

A Missed Opportunity

The Lannisters really blow it when they exclude Tyrion from their team. This is mostly Tywin's and Cersei's doing, Jamie is alone in caring about his brother.

For people who know the story of this family, seeing these images is so sad.

Tyrion has the qualities the others lack — insight and compassion — in abundance.

In the ninth episode of season 2, Blackwater, he plays a crucial role in saving the city, but people — most tragically his entire family — refuse to see it.
